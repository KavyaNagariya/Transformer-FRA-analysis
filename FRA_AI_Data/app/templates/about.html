WHAT IS FRA?

Frequency Response Analysis (FRA) is a widely used diagnostic technique for assessing the internal mechanical and electrical condition of power transformers. A low-voltage signal is injected into transformer windings across a wide frequency range (typically 20 Hz to 2 MHz), and the resulting frequency response is recorded.

Any deviation in the response pattern from expected behavior can indicate internal faults such as winding deformation, core displacement, insulation degradation, or structural changes from transportation stresses, short-circuit forces, or aging.

THE CHALLENGE

  • FRA interpretation requires highly experienced engineers to identify subtle curve variations.
  • Equipment from OMICRON, Megger, and Doble generate data in incompatible formats.
  • Lack of standardization causes inefficiencies and delayed fault identification.
  • Manual analysis leads to inconsistencies in maintenance decision-making.

OUR SOLUTION

An AI-powered diagnostic platform that automatically processes and interprets FRA data from multiple vendors. The system uses a universal data parser, CNN-based classification models, and expert system logic to deliver accurate fault detection with actionable maintenance recommendations.
